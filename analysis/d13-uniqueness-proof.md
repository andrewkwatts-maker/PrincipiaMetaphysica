## 1. The Hurwitz Theorem: Foundation

### 1.1 Statement of the Theorem

**Theorem (Hurwitz, 1898):** The only normed division algebras over the real numbers are:

| Algebra | Dimension | Properties |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **R** (Reals) | 1 | Associative, commutative, ordered |
| **C** (Complex) | 2 | Associative, commutative, algebraically closed |
| **H** (Quaternions) | 4 | Associative, non-commutative |
| **O** (Octonions) | 8 | Non-associative, non-commutative, alternative |

**Definition:** A *normed division algebra* is a real algebra A with:
1. A multiplicative identity 1
2. A norm |·| satisfying |ab| = |a||b| (composition property)
3. Every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse

### 1.2 Why This Matters for Physics

Division algebras appear throughout fundamental physics:

- **R (1D):** Real-valued observables, thermodynamics, entropy, time parameter
- **C (2D):** Quantum mechanics, worldsheet CFT, complex analysis
- **H (4D):** Lorentz group Spin(3,1) ≅ SL(2,C) ≅ SL(2,H)|_{unit}, spacetime spinors
- **O (8D):** Exceptional structures, triality, E₈, supersymmetry in D=10

**Key Insight:** The dimensions 1, 2, 4, 8 are not arbitrary - they are the ONLY dimensions admitting normed division algebra structure.

### 2.3 Proof: 3D Has No Normed Division Algebra

**Theorem:** There exists no normed division algebra of dimension 3.

**Proof:** Suppose A is a 3D normed division algebra over R.

1. A contains R as a subalgebra (multiples of identity)
2. Take a ∈ A with a ⊥ R (orthogonal to reals in the norm)
3. Consider the subalgebra generated by {1, a}
4. By the norm property: |a²| = |a|²
5. Write a² = α + βa for some α, β ∈ R
6. Then |a²|² = α² + β²|a|² but also |a²|² = |a|⁴
7. This forces β = 0 (otherwise degrees don't match)
8. So a² = α ∈ R, making {1, a} isomorphic to C or split-C
9. This 2D subalgebra leaves only 1 dimension for the rest
10. But any additional element b ⊥ {1, a} would generate a 4D space
11. **Contradiction:** 3D cannot be achieved ∎
