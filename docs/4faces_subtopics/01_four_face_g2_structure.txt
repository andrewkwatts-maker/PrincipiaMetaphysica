## Summary
The h^{1,1}=4 Kähler moduli sectors of TCS G₂ #187 are reinterpreted as 
four co-existing effective G₂ "faces" per shadow, yielding 8 total effective 
G₂ realizations across both shadows. Each face selects a different combination 
of compactified directions: (7,0) core + (3,1) leakage + (2,0) bridge remnant.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Four Kähler Moduli as "Four Dice Rolls"
- h^{1,1}=4 means four independent Kähler moduli T₁, T₂, T₃, T₄
- Each controls the size/shape of distinct 2-cycle classes in G₂
- Racetrack stabilization: V({T_i}) = Σ Λ_i exp(-a_i T_i) + Λ₀, a_i = 24/i
- Stabilization proceeds independently in each shadow

### 2. Per-Face Direction Decomposition
Each face inherits: (7,0)_core + (3,1)_leakage + (2,0)_bridge_remnant
- Face 1: T₁ >> T₂,₃,₄ → purest 7,0 G₂ + minimal leakage (OUR WORLD)
- Face 2: T₂ dominant → partial 3,1 leakage + 2,0 bridge remnant
- Face 3: T₃ dominant → different 3,1 leakage pattern  
- Face 4: T₄ dominant → balanced 2,0 retention

### 3. Leakage Coupling Strength (~0.57)
α_leak ≈ 1/√(χ_eff/b₃) = 1/√6 ≈ 0.577
- Hidden faces interact at ~57% strength compared to visible sector
- Candidates for dark matter, sterile neutrinos, ultra-light axions

## New Formulas

### Per-Face Moduli VEV
T_i^(f) = (1/a_i) ln(Λ_i/Λ₀ · χ_eff/b₃), a_i = 24/i, f=1,2,3,4

### Leakage Coupling Strength  
α_leak^(f) = 1/√(χ_eff/b₃ · exp(T_i^(f)/T_max)) ≈ 0.57 (f=2,3,4)

### Effective Extra-Dimensional Mass Scale
m_extra^(f) = M_Pl · exp(-T_i^(f)/2) ≈ TeV-PeV range

### Shadow-to-Shadow Moduli Difference
ΔT_i = T_i^(1) - T_i^(2) ≈ ln(flux_asymmetry)/a_i ~ 1/24 ≈ 0.0417

## Physical Implications
1. We live in Face 1 of Shadow 1 (minimal leakage, full SM)
2. Faces 2-4 are hidden sectors (KK modes, sterile neutrinos, axions)
3. Mirror shadow has opposite chirality + its own 4 faces
4. Total: 8 effective G₂ realizations, only 1 visible to us
