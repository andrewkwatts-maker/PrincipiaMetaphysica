Physical Interpretation

The Einstein-Hilbert action embodies the principle that matter tells spacetime how to curve, and spacetime tells matter how to move. The action is proportional to the total curvature integrated over spacetime.

Variational Principle

Varying the action with respect to the metric gμν and setting δS = 0 yields Einstein's field equations: Gμν = 8πG Tμν, where Gμν is the Einstein tensor and Tμν is the stress-energy tensor from matter.

Complete Dimensional Reduction Pathway

The Principia Metaphysica framework employs a multi-stage compactification descending from the fundamental 26-dimensional bosonic string theory down to our observed 4D spacetime:

The Complete Pathway: 26D → 13D → 7D → 6D → 4D

Each stage of dimensional reduction reveals new physical structure:

  • 26D → 13D: Bosonic string compactification (removes tachyon, establishes fermions)
  • 13D → 7D: First geometric compactification (not directly physical, intermediate step)
  • 13D → 6D: G₂ manifold compactification (establishes SO(10) GUT, 3 generations)
  • 6D → 4D: Shared T² compactification (accessible TeV-scale physics)

Stage 0: 26D → 13D (Fundamental String Compactification)

Bosonic string theory lives in 26 dimensions (critical dimension for conformal anomaly cancellation). Compactifying 13 dimensions on a suitable torus or orbifold yields the 13D PM starting point:

S26D = ∫ d26X √|G26| R26  →  S13D = M*11 ∫ d13x √|G| R13 M*11 ∝ (Vol13)-1 in string units

This first reduction is typically done via orbifolding or toroidal compactification with Wilson lines to break gauge symmetry and project out the tachyon. The resulting 13D theory has signature (12,1) and serves as the effective starting dimension for PM.

Stage 1: 13D → 6D (G₂ Compactification)

Compactify on a 7-dimensional G₂ manifold M⁷, reducing to 6D effective theory:

S13D = M*11 ∫ d13x √|G| R13  →  S6D = M64 ∫ d6x √|g6| R6 M64 = M*11 × Vol(M⁷)

The G₂ holonomy ensures N=1 supersymmetry is preserved and yields SO(10) gauge symmetry from D₅ ADE singularities in M⁷. The effective Euler characteristic χeff = 72 gives exactly 3 fermion generations via ngen = χ/24.

Stage 2: 6D → 4D (Shared T² Dimensions)

Compactify the remaining two shared extra dimensions on a 2-torus T² at the ~5 TeV scale:

S6D = M64 ∫ d6x √|g6| R6  →  S4D = MPl2 ∫ d4x √|g| R MPl2 = M64 × Vol(T²) × (warp factor)

The 4D Planck mass MPl ~ 1.2 × 1019 GeV emerges from the product of the fundamental scale M*, the volume of the G₂ manifold, and the volume of the shared T². If warping is present (flux stabilization), an additional warp factor integral appears.

Combined Formula

Putting all stages together, the 4D Planck mass is:

MPl2 = M*11 × V9 where V9 = Vol(G₂) × Vol(T²) is the total compact volume

This confirms the general Kaluza-Klein formula MPl2 = M*n+2 Vn with n=9 compact dimensions. However, V9 is not independently measurable - only the product M*11 V9 = MPl2 is constrained by observation.
