Abstract

A unified theory of gravity and the Standard Model forces originating from a 26-dimensional bulk spacetime with signature (24,2)—incorporating two time dimensions. Sp(2,R) gauge fixing projects this onto a 13D effective shadow with signature (12,1), structured as a 1 + 3 brane hierarchy (one observable + three hidden universes) with Z2 mirror symmetry.

The 13D effective Lagrangian: ℒ13D = M*11R + ΨP(iΓ∇ - m)ΨP, which upon compactification on KPneuma yields the complete 4D Lagrangian: ℒ4D = F(R,T,τ) + ℒPneuma + ℒSM + ℒhidden, where F(R,T,τ) = R + αT + βT² + γτ + δτ². The fundamental Pneuma field ΨP is an 8192-component spinor in the bulk, reducing to 64 effective components in 13D. Its condensates form the internal Calabi-Yau manifold KPneuma, while its statistics generate emergent time via the Thermal Time Hypothesis.

Three fermion generations arise topologically: χ = 72/24 = 3 (F-theory) or χ = 144/48 = 3 (26D). The framework satisfies Swampland constraints with a = √(26/13) ≈ 1.414 > √(2/3). Moduli are stabilized via V(φ) = |F|²e-aφ + κe-b/φ + μcos(φ/Rortho), combining F-term contributions, non-perturbative effects, and axionic terms from the orthogonal time.

Cosmological predictions: Dark energy equation of state w0 = -0.8528, wa = -0.95, with late-time attractor w → -1 driven by the Mashiach field—a dynamical dark energy resolution requiring no fine-tuning. Enhanced predictions: Gravitational wave dispersion from multi-time coupling η = g/EF ~ 0.1 to LISA-testable regime (~10-20 with parameter tuning); CMB bubble collision signatures from multiverse tunneling (falsifiable via Planck/CMB-S4 non-Gaussian cold spots with κ > 3+109).
