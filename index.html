Abstract

This framework presents a unified theory of gravity and the Standard Model forces based on a Kaluza-Klein compactification of a (12,1) dimensional bulk spacetime. The central postulate is that the internal 8-dimensional manifold, KPneuma, is a dynamic geometric structure formed from condensates of a fundamental fermion, ΨP. We demonstrate that KPneuma can be modeled as a homogeneous space with an SO(10) isometry group, which, upon dimensional reduction, yields a 4D Einstein-Yang-Mills-Higgs theory with SO(10) as the gauge group.

The framework introduces the Thermal Time Hypothesis (TTH), linking the flow of time to the entropy gradient of the Pneuma field. Through the mechanisms of braneworlds and Kaluza-Klein compactification, the model naturally leads to a 4D modified Myrzakulov F(R,T) gravity with a late-time cosmological attractor driven by a scalar modulus (the "Mashiach" field), providing a dynamical explanation for dark energy.
