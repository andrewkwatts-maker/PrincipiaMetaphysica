F.0 Introduction: From Brittle Precision to Robust Prediction

Several predictions of Principia Metaphysica exhibit "brittle precision"—values determined exactly by geometric or symmetry arguments that happen to lie close to, but not exactly at, experimental central values. This appendix introduces subleading dispersion parameters that transform these fragile predictions into robust ones with well-motivated theoretical uncertainty bands.

The key principle is that all dispersion parameters default to zero, recovering the leading-order precision of the geometric cascade. Non-zero values provide escape hatches for future experimental refinement, but their activation requires empirical evidence rather than a priori assumption.

F.6 Falsifiability and Constraints

The subleading dispersion framework provides clear falsification criteria:

  • θ23 < 42° or > 50°: Would require εatm beyond the theoretically motivated range, challenging the shadow symmetry mechanism.

  • ΩDM/Ωb < 4.5 or > 6.0: Would exclude all allowed Nsecond values.

Key Principle: Defaults at zero maintain current precision while providing theoretically motivated escape hatches. Evidence, not assumption, determines required looseness.
