7.8.1 Path Integral Formulation and Euclidean Action

Euclidean Action in Curved Spacetime

For a scalar field φ coupled to gravity with potential V(φ), the Euclidean action is:

SE[φ, g] = ∫ d⁴x √g [(1/2)gμν∂μφ∂νφ + V(φ) - R/(16πG)]

where g is the determinant of the metric, R is the Ricci scalar, and G is Newton's constant. For O(4)-symmetric solutions in de Sitter background, we use metric ansatz:

ds² = dξ² + ρ(ξ)² dΩ₃²

where ξ is the radial Euclidean coordinate and dΩ₃² is the metric on the unit 3-sphere.

rb = 2σ/ΔV

rb = 3σ/(4ΔV)     (gravitational correction)
