Overview: SO(10) Gauge Structure

SO(10) grand unification extends the Standard Model's SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) gauge group into a single simple group. The 45 gauge bosons of SO(10) decompose as:

Gauge Bosons Count Mass Scale Role
Standard Model (g, W±, Z, γ) 12 0 - 91 GeV Observed forces
X bosons (charge ±4/3) 12 MGUT ~ 10¹⁶ GeV Mediate B,L violation
Y bosons (charge ±1/3) 12 MGUT ~ 10¹⁶ GeV Mediate B,L violation
Additional neutral 9 MGUT ~ 10¹⁶ GeV Heavy Z', W' cousins

The X and Y bosons are responsible for proton decay via dimension-6 operators. Their discovery would confirm grand unification and validate the MGUT scale prediction.

What We Know from G₂ Geometry

✓ Geometrically Derived Properties
  • Mass Scale: MX = MY ≈ MGUT = GeV
    Derived from TCS G₂ torsion logarithms: MGUT = M* exp(Tωs/2)
  • Gauge Group: SO(10) from G₂ holonomy breaking
    45 gauge bosons total (8 gluons + 3 W/Z + 1 photon + 33 heavy)
  • Coupling Strength: αGUT = 1/ at MGUT
    All three SM couplings unify to this value (no fitting)
  • Existence: Required by SO(10) representation theory
    12 X bosons + 12 Y bosons complete the adjoint representation

What We Don't Know (Geometry Doesn't Constrain)

⚠ Theoretical Estimates
  • Decay Branching Ratios: Unknown

  • Exact Lifetime: τX,Y ~ 10-41 s (order of magnitude)
    Dimensional analysis: τ ~ ℏ/(MGUTc²) ≈ 3×10-42 s
  • Production Cross-Sections: Not computed
    Would require full matrix element calculations in 13D → 4D reduction
  • Mass Splitting: MX vs MY assumed degenerate
    Radiative corrections may split masses at percent level

✓ Experimental Strategy: Since direct production is impossible, X and Y bosons can only be detected indirectly through virtual exchange in proton decay experiments (Super-K, Hyper-K, DUNE). The predicted lifetime τp = 3.83×10³⁴ years and branching ratio BR(e+π0) = 64.2% provide smoking-gun signatures of SO(10) X,Y bosons.
